    This page will take you through some basics of html coding with the help of some friendly doggos. At the same time, we will learn some basic history and information on doggo speak culture.

    Origin

    Doge to Doggo, the doge that started it all and the video that went viral.

    In 2013 a picture posted by a Japanese Kindergarten teacher of her Shiba Inu named Kabosu turned into an internet sensation. The "mona-lisa"-esque, ambiguious facial expression of the dog seems to be the initial attraction. It sparked a global meme using comic-sans font and grammatically incorrect English. Here we will learn how to embed an image in HTML.

    Some might say that what truly made the doge phenomena take off (pun intended) was when "Doge Adventure" went viral on YouTube. With 2.2 million views, "Doge Adventure" is a cartoon of Kobasu on an adventure through the atmosphere. Here we will take a look at inserting this video into our index.html using iframe. A two minute version of video will follow.

    After the virility of "Doge Adventure" there was a lag before the dawn of the doggo. Doggo was most likely a term popularized by the Instagram account @WeRateDogs, but originally hailes from the land down under. In Australia they like to short form or make up cute nicknames for pretty well everything, and doggo seems to be part of the vernacular. One internet phrase lead to another and now we have our own Doggo Speak.

    Types of Doggo

    Since the dawn of doggo, there have been many new words created to describe different types of doggos. There are many, many different words to describe different sorts and breeds of our four legged friends, but for the purposes of this page we will stick to the most commonly used terms. We will use these various types of doggo to illustrate how to make lists in HTML. First we will start with an unordered list of doggo terms that refer to doggos by their general aesthetic and tempermant.

    • Doggo - your average everyday dog
    • Pupper - doggo youth, the puppies
    • Borker - small but loud and proud
    • Floof - much fur everywhere
    • Woofer - lorge and loud

    Next we will look at an ordered list of doggo terms that typically refer to specific breeds of doggos.

    1. Goldenboi - Golden Retriever
    2. Longboi - Greyhound (or Russian wolfhounds)
    3. Cloud - Samoyed
    4. Puggerino - Pug

    Doggo Noises

    Besides coming up with words to describe different types of doggos, the internet has also come up with some words that help us understand what kind of noise the doggo is making. The most common of these are:

    • Woof - common with lorgebois has a deep timbre
    • Bork - comes in all sizes, but often the smol doggos are known to bork
    • Awoo - almost exclusively with huskos but some other howlers too
    • Boof - the snorting, exhaling sound, often made when tracking small animals like squirrels

    Doggo Sizes

    Not to be missed are the all important adjective words that we use to affectionately describe doggos. Pictures are provided for reference.

    Long (or tall, has long body or long legs)

    Thicc (often used in reference to corgos)

    Smol (and in this case pupper as well)

    Lorge (unlike thicc or chonky this doggo is very lorge by nature)

    Chonky (similar to thicc, but less curvacious and more... chonk)

    Other Doggo Terms

    • Boop: "Affectionately poking someone(doggo) on the nose, often accompanied by saying "Boop!"
    • Snoot: "A nose or other boopable extremety, generally exaggerated to excentuate smolness."
    • Blop/blep: "When a dog forgets to put its tongue back in its mouth, causing the tip to stick out. Same thing as blep in cats."
    • Mlem: "(adjective) the sound affect it makes when a tongue exits and re-enters the mouth."
